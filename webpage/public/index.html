The lungs of Europe

The Carpathian mountains, home of Bisons

The Carpathian Mountains are home to the densest virgin forests in Europe and host over 400 unique mammal species. 60% of the population of brown bears in Europe lives in the Carpathians in Romania although their existence is regularly threatened by poachers. Recently, WWF is leading a project to reintroduce the european Bison in the Carpathians

An atypical animal rescued from prehistoric times, bison once populated European forests. But because of the expansion of agriculture, vast expanses of its habitat have disappeared and the distribution of this emblematic mammal has been massively restricted in our countries. WWF and Rewilding Europe are behind the reintroduction of the European bison in the southern Carpathians, specifically the Natura 2000 site of the Romanian Tarcu Mountains, which covers 59 000 ha.

The principle is simple: bison raised on reserves are then released into the wild after spending some time in semi-freedom, just to get used to wildlife. In 2014, the first individuals were released.

In fact, the reintroduction of bison in the Carpathians is part of a broader conservation plan that aims to protect the wildlife of this region, one of the richest in Europe. Ultimately, the goal will be to establish a network of natural areas interconnected by corridors where the natural ecosystem will be protected.
